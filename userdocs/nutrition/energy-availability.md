# Energy availability

Energy availability (EA) asks a sharper question than calories-in-vs-out: **after paying for
training, how much energy is left to run the body?**

$$ EA = \frac{\text{energy intake (kcal)} - \text{exercise energy expenditure (kcal)}}{\text{body mass reference}} \quad \text{per day} $$

Kazper composes it from three logs it already has — meals (intake), workouts (exercise kcal),
and body weight — which is why keeping all three current matters: EA is only as honest as its
worst input.

## Why it matters for endurance athletes

Chronically low EA (the research threshold zone is roughly *below ~30 kcal/kg fat-free
mass/day*, with ~45 considered fully fueled) drives the RED-S cascade: suppressed adaptation,
hormonal disruption, injury risk — the classic trap of training more while quietly under-eating.
The coach uses the EA composition to catch exactly the pattern a daily calorie summary hides:
big training days where intake didn't scale.

## Reading it in Kazper

- Look at EA across a **training block**, not single days — one low day after a race is noise,
  a low *week* in a build is a red flag.
- Cross-reference with the [wellness diary](../recovery/wellness.md) (fatigue, mood) and
  [Garmin recovery](../recovery/garmin.md) — low EA usually shows up there first.
- Race-week carb-loading intentionally spikes intake; see
  [Race prep](racing.md) for that math.
